Question 15medium

The International Date Line roughly follows:

  1. The Prime Meridian (0°)
  2. The Equator
  3. The 180° meridian (with deviations for island nations)
  4. The Tropic of Cancer
Show answer

Answer: The 180° meridian (with deviations for island nations)

The International Date Line (IDL) roughly follows the 180° meridian in the Pacific Ocean, but deviates to avoid splitting countries. Going west across the IDL, the date advances by one day. Going east, the date goes back by one day.
