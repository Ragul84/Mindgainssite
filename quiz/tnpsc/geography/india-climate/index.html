Question 5medium

El Niño affects Indian monsoon by:

  1. Strengthening the monsoon
  2. Weakening the southwest monsoon, often leading to deficient rainfall/drought in India
  3. Having no effect
  4. Causing floods in Tamil Nadu
Show answer

Answer: Weakening the southwest monsoon, often leading to deficient rainfall/drought in India

El Niño (warming of central/eastern Pacific Ocean) weakens the Walker Circulation, suppresses Indian monsoon convection, and typically leads to below-normal monsoon rainfall in India — causing droughts. La Niña has the opposite effect (stronger monsoon).

Question 6medium

Laterite soil is formed in regions with:

  1. High rainfall and low temperature
  2. High temperature and high seasonal rainfall — leaching removes silica, iron and aluminium oxides remain
  3. Low rainfall, dry conditions
  4. River alluvium deposits
Show answer

Answer: High temperature and high seasonal rainfall — leaching removes silica, iron and aluminium oxides remain

Laterite soils form in hot, humid tropical regions with high seasonal rainfall (alternating wet-dry seasons). Leaching removes silica and bases; iron and aluminium oxides remain, giving red/brick-like colour. Found in Tamil Nadu hill stations, Kerala, Karnataka, Odisha, Assam.

  4. January-February

Question 18medium

  2. Trade winds alone
  3. Jet streams alone
  4. Coriolis effect only

Answer: Differential heating of land and sea causing seasonal reversal of winds
