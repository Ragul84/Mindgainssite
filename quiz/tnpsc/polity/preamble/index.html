Question 8hard

  3. A directive principle
  4. An ordinary statute
Show answer

Question 9hard

The Kesavananda Bharati Case (1973) established the doctrine of:

  1. Equality of laws
  2. Basic Structure of the Constitution — Parliament cannot amend the basic structure
  3. Separation of powers
  4. Federal supremacy
Show answer

Answer: Basic Structure of the Constitution — Parliament cannot amend the basic structure

Kesavananda Bharati vs State of Kerala (1973): 13-judge bench held (7:6) that Parliament can amend ANY part of the Constitution under Article 368, BUT cannot alter the 'basic structure' (essential features). Basic structure includes: supremacy of Constitution, democracy, republicanism, secular character, federal character, judicial review.
