Question 55easy

  2. 0
  3. 14
  4. 5
Show answer

The phenomenon of 'osmosis' involves movement of:

  1. Water molecules from high water concentration (low solute) to low water concentration (high solute) through a semipermeable membrane
  2. Solute from high to low concentration
  3. Both solute and solvent
  4. Heat transfer
Show answer

Answer: Water molecules from high water concentration (low solute) to low water concentration (high solute) through a semipermeable membrane

Osmosis: passive movement of water (solvent) across a semipermeable membrane from the dilute solution (high water potential) to the concentrated solution (low water potential). Osmotic pressure: pressure needed to stop osmosis. Applications: kidney function, plant water absorption, food preservation (salt/sugar as preservatives by creating hypertonic conditions).

The 'tragedy of the commons' in environmental science refers to:

  1. Overexploitation of shared resources (commons) when individuals act in self-interest — fisheries, groundwater, forests, atmosphere — without collective management
  2. A pollution incident
  3. A species extinction event
  4. A nuclear disaster
Show answer

Answer: Overexploitation of shared resources (commons) when individuals act in self-interest — fisheries, groundwater, forests, atmosphere — without collective management

'Tragedy of the Commons' (Garrett Hardin, 1968): shared resources (common pool resources) are overexploited because each individual benefits from maximizing personal use while costs are shared. Examples: overfishing oceans, overgrazing commons, groundwater depletion, atmospheric pollution. Solution: privatization, regulation, or community governance (Ostrom's approach, Nobel 2009).

Question 108easy

Which gas is used to fill weather balloons?

  1. Helium (He) — lighter than air, non-flammable (safer than hydrogen which also provides lift but is explosive)
  2. Hydrogen (H₂, historically used — Hindenburg disaster 1937)
  3. Nitrogen
  4. Oxygen
Show answer

Answer: Helium (He) — lighter than air, non-flammable (safer than hydrogen which also provides lift but is explosive)

Weather balloons use helium (or sometimes hydrogen for cost). Helium density: 0.164 kg/m³ (vs air 1.225 kg/m³) — very buoyant. Helium is non-flammable (noble gas). Hydrogen provides more lift (density 0.089 kg/m³) but is highly flammable. Weather balloons ascend to ~40 km, then burst (atmospheric pressure decreases, balloon expands).

Question 109easy

The discovery of X-rays was made by:

  2. Marie Curie
  3. Michael Faraday
  4. Thomas Edison

Answer: Wilhelm Conrad Röntgen (1895, Germany) — accidentally discovered while experimenting with cathode ray tubes; Nobel Prize in Physics 1901 (first ever Nobel in Physics)

Wilhelm Röntgen discovered X-rays on November 8, 1895, while experimenting with a cathode ray tube — he noticed a fluorescent screen glowing even when shielded by black paper. Named 'X' as unknown rays. First Nobel Prize in Physics (1901). First X-ray image: his wife's hand, showing her wedding ring and bones.
