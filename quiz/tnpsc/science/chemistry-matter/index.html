Question 49medium

  2. 0°C
  3. 0°F

Show answer

The process of 'dialysis' in chemistry (as opposed to medical dialysis) refers to:

  1. Separation of colloid particles from crystalloids (smaller molecules/ions) using a semipermeable membrane — based on same principle as kidney dialysis
  2. Distillation of liquids
  3. Crystallization of solids
  4. Electrolysis of solutions
Show answer

Answer: Separation of colloid particles from crystalloids (smaller molecules/ions) using a semipermeable membrane — based on same principle as kidney dialysis

Chemical dialysis (Thomas Graham, 1861): colloid particles (1-100 nm, e.g., proteins, starch) cannot pass through a semipermeable membrane, while crystalloids (small molecules/ions) can. Used to purify colloids from impurities. Basis for medical hemodialysis — small waste molecules (urea, creatinine) pass through membrane, blood cells and proteins are retained.
