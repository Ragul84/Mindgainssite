  1. Antigens (glycoproteins) on red blood cells: Type A has A antigens, B has B antigens, AB has both, O has neither — with corresponding antibodies in plasma
  2. Hemoglobin type
  3. White blood cell count
  4. Platelet type
Show answer

Answer: Antigens (glycoproteins) on red blood cells: Type A has A antigens, B has B antigens, AB has both, O has neither — with corresponding antibodies in plasma

ABO blood groups (Karl Landsteiner, 1901): Type A: A antigens + anti-B antibodies; Type B: B antigens + anti-A antibodies; Type AB: A and B antigens + no antibodies (universal recipient); Type O: no antigens + both antibodies (universal donor). The Rh factor (+ or -) is a separate antigen.

Question 61medium

The half-life of a radioactive substance is:

  1. Time for exactly half of the radioactive atoms to decay — constant for each isotope (C-14: 5730 years, U-235: 703 million years)
  2. Time for all atoms to decay
  3. Time for substance to double
  4. Temperature-dependent
Show answer

Answer: Time for exactly half of the radioactive atoms to decay — constant for each isotope (C-14: 5730 years, U-235: 703 million years)

Half-life (t½): time for half the radioactive nuclei to decay. It is constant for each radioisotope regardless of amount or conditions. After n half-lives, amount remaining = (½)ⁿ × initial amount. Used in carbon dating (C-14, t½=5,730 y), nuclear medicine (Tc-99m, t½=6 h), and nuclear fuel assessment.

Question 65hard

The 'Reynolds number' in fluid mechanics represents:

  1. Ratio of inertial to viscous forces — low Re = laminar flow (smooth, ordered); high Re = turbulent flow (chaotic)
  2. Pressure of a fluid
  3. Density of a fluid
  4. Temperature of a fluid
Show answer

Answer: Ratio of inertial to viscous forces — low Re = laminar flow (smooth, ordered); high Re = turbulent flow (chaotic)

Reynolds number (Re = ρvD/μ): dimensionless number predicting flow patterns. Low Re (<2000): laminar flow (smooth, parallel streamlines). High Re (>4000): turbulent flow (chaotic, mixing). Transition zone: 2000-4000. Used in designing pipes, aircraft, ships, and understanding blood flow in vessels.
