  1. Respiration

Answer: Transpiration

Question 2easy

The process by which plants lose water through leaves is called:

  1. Transpiration
  2. Evaporation
  3. Respiration
  4. Photosynthesis
Show answer

Answer: Transpiration

Transpiration is the process by which plants lose water vapor through stomata (pores) in leaves. It creates a 'pull' that helps water move up from roots to leaves (transpiration pull). About 97-99% of absorbed water is transpired.

Question 3easy

A food chain always starts with:

  1. Producers (autotrophs — plants that photosynthesize)
  2. Herbivores
  3. Carnivores
  4. Decomposers
Show answer

Answer: Producers (autotrophs — plants that photosynthesize)

Food chain: Producers (plants) → Primary consumers (herbivores) → Secondary consumers (carnivores) → Tertiary consumers → Decomposers. Energy flows from producers upward; each level has only ~10% efficiency.

Question 4easy

  2. Parasitism
  3. Commensalism
  4. Competition

Question 5hard

The term 'biodiversity hotspot' was coined by:

  1. Norman Myers (1988) — areas with high species richness and endemism under significant threat of habitat loss
  2. Charles Darwin
  3. E.O. Wilson
  4. Humboldt
Show answer

Answer: Norman Myers (1988) — areas with high species richness and endemism under significant threat of habitat loss

Norman Myers coined 'biodiversity hotspot' (1988). Criteria: ≥1,500 endemic vascular plant species AND ≥70% of original habitat already destroyed. There are 36 global hotspots. India has 4: Western Ghats-Sri Lanka, Himalaya, Indo-Burma, and Sundaland (Andaman-Nicobar).

Question 6easy

The water cycle (hydrological cycle) involves:

  1. Evaporation (water→vapor), condensation (vapor→clouds), precipitation (rain/snow), surface runoff, and infiltration → continuous recycling
  2. Only evaporation
  3. Only precipitation
  4. Only river flow
Show answer

Answer: Evaporation (water→vapor), condensation (vapor→clouds), precipitation (rain/snow), surface runoff, and infiltration → continuous recycling

The water cycle: solar energy evaporates water from oceans/land → water vapor rises and condenses into clouds → precipitation (rain, snow) → some runs off (rivers), some infiltrates (groundwater), some transpires through plants → evaporates again. Continuous cycle driven by solar energy.

Question 7easy

The study of heredity and variation is called:

  1. Genetics (founded by Gregor Mendel's pea plant experiments, 1865; term coined by William Bateson, 1905)
  2. Ecology
  3. Cytology
  4. Taxonomy
Show answer

Answer: Genetics (founded by Gregor Mendel's pea plant experiments, 1865; term coined by William Bateson, 1905)

Genetics: science of heredity and variation. Gregor Mendel (1822-1884, Austrian monk) did pea plant hybridization experiments establishing laws of inheritance (1865). Term 'genetics' coined by William Bateson (1905). 'Gene' coined by Wilhelm Johannsen (1909). Chromosome theory: Morgan, Sutton, Boveri.
