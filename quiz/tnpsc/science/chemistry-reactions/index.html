Question 1medium

Rusting of iron is a chemical process called:

  1. Sublimation
  2. Corrosion (oxidation)
  3. Reduction
  4. Decomposition
Show answer

Answer: Corrosion (oxidation)

Rusting is the corrosion of iron. It is an oxidation reaction: 4Fe + 3O2 + 6H2O → 4Fe(OH)3 (hydrated iron oxide). Moisture and oxygen are needed.

Question 2easy

Rusting of iron is an example of:

  1. Oxidation (iron reacts with oxygen and water to form iron oxide)
  2. Reduction
  3. Neutralization
  4. Fermentation
Show answer

Answer: Oxidation (iron reacts with oxygen and water to form iron oxide)

Rusting is oxidation: 4Fe + 3O₂ + 6H₂O → 4Fe(OH)₃ → 2Fe₂O₃·3H₂O (hydrated iron oxide). Prevention: painting, galvanizing (zinc coating), oiling, stainless steel (iron + chromium alloy).

Question 6easy

In a chemical reaction, the law of conservation of mass states:

  1. Total mass of reactants = Total mass of products (Lavoisier, 1774)
  2. Mass increases in exothermic reactions
  3. Mass decreases in endothermic reactions
  4. Mass is not conserved in reactions
Show answer

Answer: Total mass of reactants = Total mass of products (Lavoisier, 1774)

Antoine Lavoisier (1774) established: mass is neither created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction. Total mass of reactants equals total mass of products. This is why chemical equations must be balanced.
