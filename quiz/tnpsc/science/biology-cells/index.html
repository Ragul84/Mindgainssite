Question 17medium

Mendel's Law of Segregation states that:

  1. Two alleles of a gene separate during gamete formation — each gamete carries only one allele for each trait
  2. Traits blend together
  3. Dominant allele always shows
  4. All genes are inherited together
Show answer

Answer: Two alleles of a gene separate during gamete formation — each gamete carries only one allele for each trait

Mendel's First Law: alleles (two copies of each gene in diploid organisms) segregate (separate) during gamete (sex cell) formation. Each gamete gets only one allele. At fertilization, offspring inherit one allele from each parent. Verified by Punnett square.

Question 19medium

  2. Killing viruses
  3. Disrupting DNA replication
  4. Blocking protein synthesis directly
Show answer

Answer: Inhibiting bacterial cell wall synthesis (bacteria lyse due to osmotic pressure without the wall) — does NOT affect human cells

Penicillin (and other beta-lactam antibiotics) inhibit enzymes needed to form cross-links in the bacterial cell wall (peptidoglycan). Without the wall, bacteria burst from osmotic pressure. Human cells have no cell wall, so penicillin is safe. Alexander Fleming discovered it in 1928.

  2. Bronchi
  3. Trachea
  4. Diaphragm

Answer: Alveoli (tiny air sacs — ~700 million in lungs, total surface area ~70 m²) — O₂ enters blood, CO₂ exits

Question 23medium

  3. Inner and outer ER
  4. Active and passive ER
Show answer

Answer: Rough ER (with ribosomes — protein synthesis and processing) and Smooth ER (no ribosomes — lipid synthesis, detoxification, Ca²⁺ storage)

Rough ER: has ribosomes on surface; synthesizes and processes proteins destined for export or membrane. Smooth ER: no ribosomes; synthesizes lipids/steroids, detoxifies drugs (liver SER), and stores calcium (muscle SER). ER is continuous with the nuclear envelope (perinuclear space).
