The study of living organisms and their interactions with the environment is called:

  1. Ecology
  2. Biology
  3. Zoology
  4. Entomology
Show answer

Answer: Ecology

Question 2medium

The term 'ecology' was coined by:

  1. Ernst Haeckel (1866) — from Greek 'oikos' (household) + 'logos' (study) — study of interactions between organisms and their environment
  2. Charles Darwin
  3. Gregor Mendel
  4. Aristotle
Show answer

Answer: Ernst Haeckel (1866) — from Greek 'oikos' (household) + 'logos' (study) — study of interactions between organisms and their environment

Ernst Haeckel (1866) coined 'ecology' (Ökologie in German) from Greek oikos (house/habitat) + logos (study). Ecology studies interactions between organisms and their abiotic environment. Key concepts: ecosystem, food web, energy flow, nutrient cycles, population dynamics, and biodiversity.
