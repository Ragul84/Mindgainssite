Question 4hard

The concept of 'infant industry protection' argues:

  1. New/young industries in developing countries need temporary tariff protection to grow and become internationally competitive — used by USA (19th century), Germany (Bismarck era), Japan, South Korea, and now by developing economies including India
  2. All industries need permanent protection
  3. Free trade is never beneficial
  4. Only agricultural industries need protection
Show answer

Answer: New/young industries in developing countries need temporary tariff protection to grow and become internationally competitive — used by USA (19th century), Germany (Bismarck era), Japan, South Korea, and now by developing economies including India

Infant Industry Argument (Friedrich List, 1841 — 'National System of Political Economy'): nascent industries with potential scale economies need temporary protection (tariffs, subsidies) against established foreign competitors until they achieve global competitiveness. USA protected its manufacturing (1800s-1900s) with high tariffs. South Korea's POSCO steel. Counter: protection tends to become permanent, breeds inefficiency.

Question 5hard

The 'marginal cost of funds based lending rate' (MCLR) is different from 'base rate' in that MCLR:

  1. Uses marginal cost of funds (current deposit rates at margin, not average cost) to set lending rate — more dynamic, passes monetary policy changes faster to borrowers than base rate (which used average cost)
  2. Is the same as base rate
  3. Is set by government not banks
  4. Is only for home loans
Show answer

Answer: Uses marginal cost of funds (current deposit rates at margin, not average cost) to set lending rate — more dynamic, passes monetary policy changes faster to borrowers than base rate (which used average cost)

MCLR (April 2016): replaces Base Rate (April 2010). Base Rate used average cost of funds (lagged, slow to change). MCLR uses: marginal cost of deposits (current rates, not historical average), CRR maintenance cost, operating cost, and tenor premium. Changed monthly (MCLR) vs quarterly (Base Rate). Since EBLR (2019): retail and MSME loans linked to external benchmark (repo rate) — fastest transmission.
