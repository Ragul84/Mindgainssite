  3. 2000
  4. 1990

Question 4medium

The term 'dumping' in international trade means:

  1. Exporting goods to a foreign market at prices below production cost or below domestic price — considered unfair trade practice; subject to anti-dumping duties under WTO rules
  2. Disposing industrial waste
  3. Selling expired goods
  4. Selling goods at higher price abroad
Show answer

Answer: Exporting goods to a foreign market at prices below production cost or below domestic price — considered unfair trade practice; subject to anti-dumping duties under WTO rules

Dumping: selling exported goods below cost of production (or below home market price) to gain market share in foreign country. Damages domestic industries in the importing country. WTO's Anti-Dumping Agreement: allows importing country to impose anti-dumping duties to offset price difference. India frequently imposes anti-dumping duties on Chinese goods.

Question 5medium

The term 'Current Account' in Balance of Payments includes:

  1. Trade in goods (merchandise trade balance), trade in services (services account), primary income (wages, dividends, interest received/paid), and secondary income (remittances, transfers)
  2. Only goods trade
  3. Only capital flows (FDI, FPI)
  4. Only loans and grants
Show answer

Answer: Trade in goods (merchandise trade balance), trade in services (services account), primary income (wages, dividends, interest received/paid), and secondary income (remittances, transfers)

Current Account (CA) = Merchandise trade (visible trade) + Services trade (invisible trade: IT, tourism, shipping) + Primary Income (factor income: wages, profits, dividends, interest) + Secondary Income (current transfers: remittances, government grants). India's CA deficit largely reflects merchandise trade deficit (imports > exports), partially offset by services surplus and remittances.

Question 6hard

The IMF's Special Drawing Rights (SDR) is:

  1. International reserve asset created by IMF — not a currency; its value is based on a basket of 5 currencies (USD, EUR, CNY, JPY, GBP); SDRs allocated to member countries in proportion to quotas
  2. A crypto currency
  3. IMF's common currency
  4. Gold standard replacement
Show answer

Answer: International reserve asset created by IMF — not a currency; its value is based on a basket of 5 currencies (USD, EUR, CNY, JPY, GBP); SDRs allocated to member countries in proportion to quotas

SDR (1969): IMF-created international reserve asset to supplement member countries' official reserves. Based on basket: USD 41.73%, EUR 30.93%, CNY 10.92%, JPY 8.33%, GBP 8.09% (2022 weights). Not a currency; cannot be used in private transactions. Countries can exchange SDRs for hard currencies. During COVID-19: IMF allocated SDR 456.5 billion ($650 billion) to member countries.
