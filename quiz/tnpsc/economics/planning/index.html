  2. When price rises, demand rises
  3. Demand is always constant
  4. Supply determines demand
Show answer

Answer: When price of a good rises, quantity demanded falls (inverse relationship between price and quantity demanded)

Law of Demand (Alfred Marshall): price and quantity demanded have an inverse relationship — as price rises, quantity demanded falls (consumers buy less; some switch to substitutes). Exceptions (Giffen goods, Veblen goods, necessities, wartime hoarding). Demand curve slopes downward.

Question 16hard

The term 'multiplier effect' in economics means:

  1. An initial change in spending leads to a larger final change in income — if multiplier = 2, then Rs 100 crore investment → Rs 200 crore income increase
  2. Prices multiplying during inflation
  3. Population growth multiplier
  4. Export multiplier only
Show answer

Answer: An initial change in spending leads to a larger final change in income — if multiplier = 2, then Rs 100 crore investment → Rs 200 crore income increase

Keynesian Multiplier: k = 1/(1-MPC) = 1/MPS. Where MPC = Marginal Propensity to Consume (fraction of extra income spent). If MPC = 0.8, k = 1/(1-0.8) = 5. An injection of Rs 100 crore in government spending creates Rs 500 crore in total income (as each round of spending becomes income for someone else).
