Question 392medium

Assertion: The binding energy per nucleon increases with the increase in mass number for elements up to iron. Reason: Nuclear forces are short-range forces.

  1. Both Assertion and Reason are true and Reason is the correct explanation of Assertion.
  2. Both Assertion and Reason are true but Reason is not the correct explanation of Assertion.
  3. Assertion is true but Reason is false.
  4. Assertion is false but Reason is true.
Show answer

Answer: Both Assertion and Reason are true but Reason is not the correct explanation of Assertion.

The binding energy per nucleon increases up to iron due to the increasing strong nuclear force overcoming electrostatic repulsion. Beyond iron, the increase in electrostatic repulsion due to a larger number of protons causes the binding energy per nucleon to decrease. Nuclear forces are indeed short-range, which explains why larger nuclei are less stable than medium-sized ones like iron, but it doesn't directly explain the initial increase in binding energy per nucleon.
