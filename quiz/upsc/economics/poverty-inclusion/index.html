Question 7medium

The 'poverty trap' is a situation where:

  1. Poor people cannot escape poverty because they lack resources to invest in health, education, or productive assets — low income → low savings → low investment → low income (vicious cycle)
  2. Only high taxation causes poverty
  3. Poverty is self-fulfilling prophecy due to geography
  4. Lack of democracy causes poverty
Show answer

Answer: Poor people cannot escape poverty because they lack resources to invest in health, education, or productive assets — low income → low savings → low investment → low income (vicious cycle)

Poverty Trap (Jeffrey Sachs): poor people/countries stuck in poverty because: too poor to save/invest → capital doesn't accumulate → productivity stays low → income stays low. Solutions: 'Big Push' (massive external aid to break the cycle), conditional cash transfers, microfinance. Counter-view (Daron Acemoglu, James Robinson): institutions matter more than external shocks.

The concept of 'disguised unemployment' is different from 'seasonal unemployment' in that:

  1. Disguised unemployment: excess labor with zero marginal product (mainly in agriculture, year-round). Seasonal unemployment: workers unemployed during off-season (only part of year)
  2. They are the same
  3. Seasonal is worse than disguised
  4. Disguised unemployment only in cities
Show answer

Answer: Disguised unemployment: excess labor with zero marginal product (mainly in agriculture, year-round). Seasonal unemployment: workers unemployed during off-season (only part of year)

Disguised Unemployment (structural): workers appear employed but contribute zero output (farm has 5 workers when 3 suffice). Zero marginal product. Removing 2 workers doesn't reduce output. Seasonal Unemployment: workers unemployed part of year (farmers during off-season, tourism workers in lean season). Cyclic unemployment: due to recession. Frictional: between jobs.
