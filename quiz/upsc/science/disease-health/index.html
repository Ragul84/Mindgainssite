  1. 4 kcal/gram
  2. 9 kcal/gram
  3. 7 kcal/gram
  4. 2 kcal/gram
Show answer

Answer: 4 kcal/gram

Question 2medium

Eutrophication of water bodies is caused by:

  1. Excess nutrients (nitrates, phosphates from fertilizers/sewage) causing algal bloom, oxygen depletion, and fish death
  2. Excess salt
  3. Industrial metal discharge
  4. Oil spills
Show answer

Answer: Excess nutrients (nitrates, phosphates from fertilizers/sewage) causing algal bloom, oxygen depletion, and fish death

Eutrophication: excess nutrients (N, P) from agricultural runoff and sewage promote algal bloom. When algae die, bacterial decomposition consumes dissolved oxygen (hypoxia), creating 'dead zones' where fish cannot survive.

Question 3medium

The process of nitrogen fixation is carried out by:

  1. Nitrogen-fixing bacteria (Rhizobium in legume root nodules, Azotobacter free-living in soil, Cyanobacteria in water/soil) — convert N₂ gas to NH₃
  2. Plants directly
  3. Lightning (some, but minor)
  4. Only Haber Process industrially
Show answer

Answer: Nitrogen-fixing bacteria (Rhizobium in legume root nodules, Azotobacter free-living in soil, Cyanobacteria in water/soil) — convert N₂ gas to NH₃

Biological nitrogen fixation: bacteria reduce N₂ → NH₃ using enzyme nitrogenase. Rhizobium (symbiotic with legumes: peas, beans, soy, peanuts). Azotobacter (free-living aerobic). Cyanobacteria (e.g., Anabaena). Haber-Bosch process (industrial, 400-500°C, 150-200 atm, Fe catalyst): produces artificial fertilizers.
