Question 4medium

Bt cotton is a genetically modified crop that produces:

  1. Bt toxin (from Bacillus thuringiensis) in the plant — kills bollworm insects that eat the plant; reduces pesticide use
  2. More yield without any modification
  3. Nitrogen fertilizer
  4. Drought resistance
Show answer

Answer: Bt toxin (from Bacillus thuringiensis) in the plant — kills bollworm insects that eat the plant; reduces pesticide use

Bt cotton contains a gene from Bacillus thuringiensis bacteria that produces Cry proteins (Bt toxin) harmful to lepidopteran insects (bollworms). Bt crops have significantly reduced pesticide use in India. Bt cotton was first approved in India in 2002.
