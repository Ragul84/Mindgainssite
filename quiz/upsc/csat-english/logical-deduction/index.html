What does the expression 'to play devil's advocate' mean?

  2. To argue against a position you may agree with, to explore the issue thoroughly
  3. To be a skilled debater
  4. To refuse to participate in a discussion
Show answer

Answer: To argue against a position you may agree with, to explore the issue thoroughly

'Play devil's advocate': to argue against a position not because you necessarily disagree, but to test the argument or explore the issue from all sides. Question 3medium

What is a 'non sequitur'?

  1. A logical conclusion
  2. A statement that does not follow logically from what precedes it
  3. A type of poetry
  4. A rhetorical question
Show answer

Answer: A statement that does not follow logically from what precedes it

Non sequitur (Latin: 'it does not follow'): a conclusion or statement that does not logically follow from the previous statement. In logic, it is a fallacy where the conclusion doesn't connect to the premises. In general use: any irrelevant or absurd response. 'He said he loves coffee, therefore he must be a scientist' — non sequitur.

Question 5medium

What is the difference between 'imply' and 'infer'?

  1. They mean exactly the same thing
  2. 'Imply' means to hint or suggest (speaker/writer); 'infer' means to conclude from evidence (listener/reader)
  3. 'Imply' is used for speech; 'infer' for writing
  4. 'Infer' is formal; 'imply' is informal
Show answer

Answer: 'Imply' means to hint or suggest (speaker/writer); 'infer' means to conclude from evidence (listener/reader)

Imply: to suggest without directly stating (done by the communicator). 'She implied she was unhappy.' Infer: to draw a conclusion from evidence (done by the audience). 'I inferred from her tone that she was unhappy.' Memory tip: 'Imply' = hint (put OUT); 'Infer' = deduce (take IN).
