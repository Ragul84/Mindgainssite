Question 51hard

Considering the impact of the North Atlantic Oscillation (NAO) on regional climate patterns, which of the following scenarios is most likely to occur during a strongly positive NAO phase?

  1. Increased precipitation and milder winters in Northern Europe, coupled with drier conditions in Southern Europe and the Mediterranean.
  2. Colder, snowier winters in Northern Europe, and wetter, milder conditions across Southern Europe.

Question 52hard

The Atacama Desert is one of the driest places on Earth, primarily due to the combined influence of the cold Peru (Humboldt) Current and the Andes Mountains. Which of the following atmospheric phenomena is most directly responsible for maintaining this extreme aridity, given these geographical factors?

  1. The formation of adiabatic lapse rates over the high Andes, preventing moisture advection from the Amazon basin.
  2. The persistent presence of a stable atmospheric inversion layer, trapping cool, moist air at lower altitudes and preventing convection.
  3. The strong Coriolis effect deflecting moisture-laden air masses away from the coastal regions.
  4. The seasonal migration of the Intertropical Convergence Zone (ITCZ) away from the subtropical latitudes.
Show answer

Answer: The persistent presence of a stable atmospheric inversion layer, trapping cool, moist air at lower altitudes and preventing convection.

The cold Peru Current cools the air above it, creating a stable atmospheric inversion layer. This prevents vertical air movement and cloud formation, trapping any moisture at low levels as fog.
