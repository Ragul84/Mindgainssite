  4. 18

Answer: 10

All numbers are even, but checking factors: 4=2², 8=2³, 14=2×7, 16=2⁴, 18=2×9. Another pattern: 8,14,16,18 are not multiples of 4 (well 8,16 are). Wait: 4,8,16 are powers of 2. 10,14,18 are not. But 10 is the outlier if looking at multiples: 4,8,12,16,20 (multiples of 4) — but 10,14,18 are not. The simplest: these should be even numbers divisible by 2 but not 4 or vice versa. Looking at it differently: 8,10,14,16,18 differ by 2,4,2,2... actually 4,8,10,14,16,18: differences are 4,2,4,2,2. 10 is not part of a consistent pattern. Or: remove 10 as it's not a product of 2 consecutive even numbers or... 10=2×5; others: 4=2×2, 8=2×4, 14=2×7, 16=2×8, 18=2×9. 5 is the only prime that gives even result without a '2'. Alternatively 10 is out because the series could be: 4,8,14,16,18 where 10 is not fitting. Best answer: 10.
