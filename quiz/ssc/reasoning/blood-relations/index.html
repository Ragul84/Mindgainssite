  3. Rapid
  4. Speed
Show answer

Answer: Slow

Question 2hard

Statement: Some apples are oranges. Some oranges are grapes. Conclusion I: Some apples are grapes. Conclusion II: No apple is a grape.

  1. Only I follows
  2. Only II follows
  3. Either I or II follows
  4. Neither follows
Show answer

Answer: Either I or II follows

From 'some apples are oranges' and 'some oranges are grapes', we cannot determine the relationship between apples and grapes definitively. It's possible some apples are grapes (if the overlapping oranges are those both). It's equally possible no apples are grapes. Since both are equally possible and one must be true, 'Either I or II follows'.
