Question 4medium

Which blood group is called the 'universal donor'?

  1. A+
  2. B+
  3. O-
  4. AB+
Show answer

Answer: O-

O- (O negative) is the universal donor for red blood cells as it can be given to patients of any blood group.

Question 5medium

Which blood group is the 'universal recipient'?

  1. A+
  2. AB+
  3. O+
  4. B+
Show answer

Answer: AB+

AB+ (AB positive) is the universal recipient as people with this blood group can receive blood from any donor group.

Question 6medium

Which hormone is produced by the pancreas to control blood sugar?

  1. Adrenaline
  2. Thyroxine
  3. Insulin
  4. Oestrogen
Show answer

Answer: Insulin

Insulin is produced by the beta cells of the pancreatic islets. It regulates blood glucose by allowing cells to take up glucose. Deficiency of insulin causes diabetes.

Question 7medium

Photosynthesis takes place in which organelle?

  1. Mitochondria
  2. Nucleus
  3. Chloroplast
  4. Ribosome
Show answer

Answer: Chloroplast

Photosynthesis occurs in chloroplasts, which contain the green pigment chlorophyll. Only plant cells and some protists have chloroplasts.

Question 8medium

The number of chromosomes in a normal human body cell is:

  1. 23
  2. 44
  3. 46
  4. 48
Show answer

Answer: 46

Normal human body cells have 46 chromosomes (23 pairs). Sperm and egg cells have 23 chromosomes each (haploid).
