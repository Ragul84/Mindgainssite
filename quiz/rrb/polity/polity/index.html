Question 1easy

The Indian Constitution was adopted on:

  1. 15 August 1947
  2. 26 November 1949
  3. 26 January 1950
  4. 1 January 1949
Show answer

Answer: 26 November 1949

The Indian Constitution was adopted by the Constituent Assembly on 26 November 1949 (Constitution Day/Samvidhan Divas). It came into force on 26 January 1950 (Republic Day). The Constituent Assembly took 2 years, 11 months, 18 days.

Question 8easy

Article 14 of the Indian Constitution guarantees:

  2. Right to Equality before law and Equal protection of laws
  3. Right to Life and Personal Liberty
  4. Right against Exploitation

  4. Article 16

Question 10easy

Article 21 guarantees:

  1. Freedom of speech
  2. Protection of life and personal liberty — 'No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law'
  3. Right to property
  4. Right to education
Show answer

Answer: Protection of life and personal liberty — 'No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law'

Article 21 is one of the most fundamental rights. Through judicial interpretation, it now covers: right to privacy, right to livelihood, right to health, right to clean environment, right to speedy trial, right to dignity, etc. Article 21A guarantees free education for children 6-14 years.

Question 11medium

The Right to Education (Article 21A) was added by the:

  1. 42nd Amendment
  2. 44th Amendment
  3. 86th Amendment Act, 2002
  4. 93rd Amendment
Show answer

Answer: 86th Amendment Act, 2002

The 86th Constitutional Amendment Act (2002) inserted Article 21A: 'The State shall provide free and compulsory education to all children of the age of six to fourteen years.' The Right to Education Act 2009 gives effect to this.

Question 12easy

Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSP) are in:

  1. Part III (Articles 12-35)
  2. Part IV (Articles 36-51)
  3. Part V
  4. Part VI
Show answer

Answer: Part IV (Articles 36-51)

DPSPs are in Part IV (Articles 36–51). They are non-justiciable (cannot be enforced by court), but they are 'fundamental to governance.' Inspired by Irish Constitution and the UN's Universal Declaration of Human Rights.

Question 13medium

Fundamental Duties were added to the Constitution by the:

  1. 42nd Amendment Act, 1976 (Part IVA, Article 51A)
  2. 44th Amendment
  3. 73rd Amendment
  4. 86th Amendment
Show answer

Answer: 42nd Amendment Act, 1976 (Part IVA, Article 51A)

The 42nd Amendment Act (1976) added Fundamental Duties (Part IVA, Article 51A) with 10 duties based on the recommendation of the Swaran Singh Committee. The 86th Amendment (2002) added the 11th duty — to provide opportunities for education to children aged 6-14.

Answer: 11

There are currently 11 Fundamental Duties under Article 51A. Originally 10 were added by the 42nd Amendment (1976). The 86th Amendment (2002) added the 11th duty: for parents/guardians to provide opportunities for education to children aged 6-14.

Question 15easy

The Indian Parliament consists of:

  1. Only Lok Sabha
  2. Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha
  3. President, Lok Sabha, and Rajya Sabha
  4. Lok Sabha, Rajya Sabha, and Supreme Court
Show answer

Answer: President, Lok Sabha, and Rajya Sabha

The Indian Parliament consists of the President + Lok Sabha (House of the People) + Rajya Sabha (Council of States). Article 79. The President is an integral part of Parliament (though not a member of either House).

Question 16easy

The term of the Lok Sabha is:

  1. 4 years
  2. 5 years (can be extended during National Emergency)
  3. 6 years
  4. 3 years
Show answer

Answer: 5 years (can be extended during National Emergency)

The normal term of the Lok Sabha is 5 years from the date of its first sitting after a general election (Article 83). During a National Emergency (Article 352), Parliament can extend its term by 1 year at a time by law.

Question 17medium

The Rajya Sabha is a permanent body because:

  1. Its members serve for life
  2. It is never dissolved — only 1/3 of its members retire every 2 years
  3. It has more powers than Lok Sabha
  4. Its members are elected for 10 years
Show answer

Answer: It is never dissolved — only 1/3 of its members retire every 2 years

Rajya Sabha is a permanent body (Article 83) — it is never dissolved. Each member serves a 6-year term, and 1/3 of its members retire every 2 years. Currently has 245 members (238 elected + 12 nominated by President for expertise in arts, science, literature, social service).

Question 18easy

A Money Bill can only be introduced in:

  1. Rajya Sabha
  2. Lok Sabha (Article 110)
  3. Either House
  4. Joint sitting of both Houses
Show answer

Answer: Lok Sabha (Article 110)

A Money Bill (Article 110) can only be introduced in the Lok Sabha. After Lok Sabha passes it, it is sent to Rajya Sabha which can only make recommendations (not amend/reject). If no action in 14 days, it is deemed passed. Lok Sabha has final say on Money Bills.

Question 19easy

The Speaker of the Lok Sabha is elected by:

  1. President of India
  2. Members of Lok Sabha from among themselves
  3. Members of both Houses
  4. Prime Minister
Show answer

Answer: Members of Lok Sabha from among themselves

The Speaker of the Lok Sabha is elected by the members of the Lok Sabha from among themselves (Article 93). The Speaker maintains order, presides over proceedings, and is the guardian of the powers and privileges of the House.

Question 20easy

  1. Elected by Lok Sabha members
  2. President of India — who appoints the leader of the majority party/coalition in Lok Sabha
  3. Rajya Sabha
  4. Electoral College
Show answer

Answer: President of India — who appoints the leader of the majority party/coalition in Lok Sabha

The President appoints the Prime Minister (Article 75). Conventionally, the President appoints the leader of the party/coalition that commands majority in the Lok Sabha. The PM is the head of the Council of Ministers and real executive head.

Question 21easy

The Governor of a state is appointed by:

  1. State Legislature
  2. Chief Minister of the state
  3. President of India
  4. Prime Minister
Show answer

Answer: President of India

The Governor is appointed by the President of India (Article 155). The Governor is the constitutional head of the state (like President at national level) and acts as the agent/representative of the Centre in the state.

Question 22easy

A State Legislative Assembly (Vidhan Sabha) member must be at least:

  1. 21 years old
  2. 25 years old
  3. 30 years old
  4. 18 years old
Show answer

Answer: 25 years old

To be a member of a State Legislative Assembly (Vidhan Sabha), a person must be at least 25 years old (Article 173). To vote, the minimum age is 18 years (reduced from 21 by the 61st Amendment, 1988).

Question 23easy

The 73rd Constitutional Amendment Act (1992) related to:

  1. Parliament elections
  2. Panchayati Raj institutions — gave constitutional status to 3-tier system
  3. Urban local bodies
  4. Financial relations between states and centre
Show answer

Answer: Panchayati Raj institutions — gave constitutional status to 3-tier system

73rd Amendment (1992) added Part IX (Articles 243-243O) and 11th Schedule to the Constitution, giving constitutional status to Panchayati Raj. Mandated 3-tier system (Village, Intermediate, District Panchayats), elections, reservation for SC/ST/women (33%), 5-year term.

Question 24easy

The minimum age for voting in India is:

  1. 21 years
  2. 18 years (changed from 21 by 61st Amendment, 1988)
  3. 20 years
  4. 25 years
Show answer

Answer: 18 years (changed from 21 by 61st Amendment, 1988)

The voting age was reduced from 21 to 18 years by the 61st Constitutional Amendment Act (1988). This was done to ensure greater youth participation in democracy. India has ~950 million eligible voters (2024).
