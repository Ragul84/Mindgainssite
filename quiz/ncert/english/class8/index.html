  3. The committee has decided on the new rules.

Show answer

Answer: The committee has decided on the new rules.

Subject-verb agreement means that the verb must agree in number with its subject. In option A, 'Each' is singular, so the verb should be 'has' (Each of the students has submitted...). In option B, when 'neither...nor' connects two subjects, the verb agrees with the subject closer to it. 'Teachers' is plural, so the verb should be 'are' (Neither the principal nor the teachers are present). In option C, 'committee' is a collective noun. When it acts as a single unit, it takes a singular verb, which 'has decided' is. In option D, 'A number of' is followed by a plural noun and takes a plural verb, so it should be 'are missing' (A number of books are missing...). Therefore, option C is the only grammatically correct sentence.
