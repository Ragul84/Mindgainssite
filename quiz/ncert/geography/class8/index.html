Question 2easy

Which of the following factors is NOT responsible for soil formation?

  1. Parent rock
  2. Climate
  3. Time
  4. Distance from the sea
Show answer

Answer: Distance from the sea

Soil formation is a complex process influenced by several factors. Parent rock determines the chemical composition and texture of the soil. Climate (temperature, rainfall) affects weathering and decomposition. Time allows for the development of soil profiles. Organisms (flora, fauna, microorganisms) and topography (relief) are also crucial. Distance from the sea, while influencing climate, is not a direct factor in the chemical and physical breakdown of parent material into soil.

  2. Overgrazing
  3. Afforestation
  4. Overuse of chemical fertilisers
Show answer

Answer: Afforestation

Soil degradation refers to the decline in soil quality. Deforestation (cutting down trees), overgrazing (excessive grazing by livestock), and overuse of chemical fertilisers all contribute to soil degradation by removing protective cover, compacting soil, or altering its chemical composition. Afforestation, which is the planting of trees, helps in soil conservation by preventing erosion and improving soil structure, thus it is not a cause of soil degradation.
