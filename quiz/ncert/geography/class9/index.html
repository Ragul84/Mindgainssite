Question 34medium

Which of the following is the most significant factor influencing the climate of India, leading to the distinct seasonal reversal of wind systems?

  1. The Himalayas
  2. The Indian Ocean
  3. The Tropic of Cancer
  4. The Peninsular Plateau
Show answer

Answer: The Himalayas

The Himalayas play a crucial role in influencing India's climate. They act as a barrier, preventing cold winds from Central Asia from entering the subcontinent, thus keeping winters milder than they would otherwise be. More importantly, they are responsible for trapping the monsoon winds, forcing them to shed their moisture over the Indian subcontinent, leading to the distinct seasonal reversal of wind systems (monsoons) that characterize India's climate. While the Indian Ocean provides moisture, and the Tropic of Cancer defines the tropical zone, and the Peninsular Plateau influences local weather, the Himalayas are the primary factor for the overall monsoon type of climate.
