Which of the following events is considered a major turning point in the process of creating a nation-state in Europe, leading to the establishment of a unified German Empire?

  1. The Frankfurt Parliament of 1848
  2. The Congress of Vienna of 1815
  3. The Franco-Prussian War of 1870-1871
  4. The Greek War of Independence

The Franco-Prussian War (1870-1871) was a decisive conflict that led to the unification of Germany under Prussian leadership. Question 35medium

The 'Great Depression' of the 1930s had a profound impact on the global economy. Which of the following was a primary cause of this economic downturn?

  1. The rise of protectionist trade policies in Europe
  2. The collapse of the US stock market in 1929
  3. The widespread adoption of the gold standard by all major economies
  4. The end of World War I and demobilization
Show answer

Answer: The collapse of the US stock market in 1929

The Great Depression was primarily triggered by the collapse of the US stock market in 1929, often referred to as 'Black Tuesday'. This event led to a widespread panic, a sharp decline in consumer confidence and spending, bank failures, and a contraction of credit. While protectionist policies and the gold standard played a role in exacerbating and prolonging the depression, the initial and most significant catalyst was the stock market crash. The end of World War I preceded the depression by over a decade and was not its direct cause.
