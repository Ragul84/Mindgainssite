Which of the following terms was used in the medieval period to refer to a foreigner, someone who was not part of that society or culture?

  1. Hindustan
  2. Jati
  3. Pardesi
  4. Shreni
Show answer

Answer: Pardesi

In the medieval period, the term 'Pardesi' was used to describe a foreigner, someone who was not from the same village or community. The meaning of 'foreigner' has changed over time; in earlier times, it could even mean someone from a different village, not necessarily a different country. 'Hindustan' referred to a geographical region, 'Jati' to a sub-caste, and 'Shreni' to a guild of merchants or artisans.

Question 16easy

The Gonds, a prominent tribal community, practiced shifting cultivation. What is another name for shifting cultivation?

  1. Terrace farming
  2. Jhum cultivation
  3. Commercial farming
  4. Plantation agriculture
Show answer

Answer: Jhum cultivation

Shifting cultivation is also known as 'jhum cultivation' or 'slash-and-burn agriculture'. In this method, a patch of forest land is cleared by cutting trees and burning the vegetation. The ashes fertilize the soil, and crops are grown for a few years until the soil loses its fertility. Then, the cultivators move to a new patch of land. Terrace farming is practiced on hillsides, commercial farming is for profit, and plantation agriculture involves growing a single cash crop on a large estate.
