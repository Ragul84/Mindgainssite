Question 9medium

Which of the following statements is true regarding the flow of energy in an ecosystem?

  2. Energy flows bidirectionally between producers and consumers, with some loss at each trophic level.
  3. Energy flows unidirectionally from producers to consumers, and a significant portion is lost as heat at each trophic level.
  4. Energy is created by producers and completely consumed by decomposers.
Show answer

Answer: Energy flows unidirectionally from producers to consumers, and a significant portion is lost as heat at each trophic level.

Energy flow in an ecosystem is unidirectional, meaning it moves from producers (autotrophs) to consumers (heterotrophs) and eventually to decomposers. At each transfer from one trophic level to the next, a significant amount of energy (typically around 90%) is lost as heat to the environment, following the second law of thermodynamics. Energy is not recycled back to producers in the same way matter is.
